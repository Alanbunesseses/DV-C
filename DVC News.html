Technology

Australia's Parliament has recently passed a landmark bill to ban children under 16 from social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, Reddit, and X. The aim is to protect kids from the physical and mental health risks associated with excessive social media use. Social media companies will face fines up to 50 million AUD if they fail to enforce this age restriction.

Policy

In policy news, Australia is making headlines with its new social media ban for children. The Online Safety Amendment (Social Media Minimum Age) Act 2024 aims to tackle online bullying and harmful content. Critics argue it might have unintended consequences, needing more time for assessment.
